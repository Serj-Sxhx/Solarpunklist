# SolarpunkDigest — Newsletter App Build Prompt

## What You're Building

A single-user newsletter app called **SolarpunkDigest** that automatically researches breakthroughs in the solarpunk space every day (3-5 high-quality items), presents them in an admin dashboard for curation, then generates and sends a polished email digest to subscribers.

This extends an existing product (SolarpunkList, a directory of regenerative communities hosted at solarpunklist.com). The newsletter features a "Community Spotlight" at the top of each issue pulled from the SolarpunkList discovery pipeline, followed by curated news items across solarpunk categories.

The key workflow: **AI researches daily → items accumulate → admin curates → AI generates digest → email sends.**

There are two deliverables:
1. **The newsletter backend** (Replit app): research engine, admin dashboard, digest generation, email sending
2. **The signup page** (on SolarpunkList): a beautiful `/newsletter` route at solarpunklist.com with AI-generated background video, email capture, and success redirect

## Stack (Replit-Native)

- **Framework:** Next.js 14+ (App Router)
- **Database:** Replit's built-in PostgreSQL (Neon-powered, access via `DATABASE_URL` env var)
- **ORM:** Prisma (works well with Replit's Postgres + gives you migrations)
- **Styling:** Tailwind CSS + shadcn/ui
- **AI Research:** Exa Search API (semantic web search + content extraction)
- **AI Generation:** Anthropic Claude API (digest copywriting)
- **AI Images (fallback):** fal.ai (generate a hero image when no source image can be scraped)
- **Email Sending:** Resend (transactional email with verified domain)
- **Scheduler:** Replit's built-in cron OR a `/api/cron/trigger` endpoint secured with `CRON_SECRET`
- **Deployment:** Replit Deployments

1. Add Resend's DKIM records (3 CNAME records)
2. Add Resend's SPF record (TXT)
3. Add Resend's DMARC record (TXT)

The app should include a preflight check script (`/api/admin/preflight`) that hits the Resend API to confirm domain verification status before allowing sends.

## Technology Readiness Level (TRL) Scale

Every discovered item gets a TRL rating. Claude assigns this based on the evidence in the article. The full scale:

| TRL | Name | What It Means | Example |
|-----|------|---------------|---------|
| 1 | Basic Principles Observed | Paper studies, theoretical research | "Researchers publish paper on mycelium-based circuit boards" |
| 2 | Technology Concept Formulated | Practical application hypothesized, analytical studies | "Team proposes using atmospheric water generators powered by solar for desert communities" |
| 3 | Experimental Proof of Concept | Lab validation of individual elements | "Lab demo shows LoRa mesh can relay soil data over 15km in hilly terrain" |
| 4 | Lab Validation | Components integrated, low-fidelity prototype in lab | "Breadboard prototype of edge AI chip runs plant disease detection at 0.5W" |
| 5 | Relevant Environment Validation | Tested in simulated or partially real conditions | "Solar microgrid controller tested in simulated village load conditions" |
| 6 | Prototype Demo | High-fidelity prototype in relevant environment | "3D-printed earthen wall module withstands 6 months of weather testing" |
| 7 | Operational Prototype | Near-final system in real environment | "Autonomous drone reforestation system plants 10,000 seeds across 50 hectares in field trial" |
| 8 | System Qualified | Proven to work in final form | "Iron-air battery system passes utility-grade certification for community storage" |
| 9 | Proven Deployment | Fully operational in real mission/commercial use | "Community solar microgrid powers 200 homes in rural Kenya for 2+ years" |

**Frontier scoring guidance for Claude:**
- High frontier (0.8-1.0): First-of-its-kind at any TRL. A TRL 1-3 paper on something nobody's tried before. A TRL 7 deployment that breaks a long-standing barrier. Unexpected crossovers between fields.
- Medium frontier (0.4-0.7): Meaningful advancement within a known field. New open-source implementation of existing tech. Notable scale-up or cost reduction.
- Low frontier (0.0-0.3): Incremental improvement. Another solar farm goes online. Known tech deployed in a new location. Still worth cataloguing, not worth starring.

## Core Flow

### 1. Daily Research Cron (`/api/cron/research`)

Triggered daily (e.g. every day at 6am UTC). Secured with `CRON_SECRET` header.

```
POST /api/cron/research
Authorization: Bearer $CRON_SECRET
```

**What it does:**
1. Execute 10 Exa searches — one query per subcategory (Solar Microgrids, Wind Turbines, Rainwater Harvesting, Edge AI Compute, Permaculture Design, Battery Storage, LoRa/Mesh Networks, CNC & 3D Printing, Autonomous Drones, IoT Sensor Arrays). Vary the exact query wording each day from the pool.
2. Use Exa's `/contents` endpoint to pull full text + images from each result
3. Deduplicate against existing `DiscoveredItem.sourceUrl` entries
4. For each new unique item, call Claude to:
   - Generate a 2-3 sentence summary
   - Assign one or more subcategory tags from the 10 subcategories
   - Generate additional freeform tags
   - Rate relevance (0-1 score)
   - **Assign a TRL level (1-9)** based on the technology's maturity described in the article (see TRL scale below)
   - **Assess a "frontier score" (0-1)** — how novel, cutting-edge, or paradigm-shifting is this? A TRL 1-3 breakthrough in a new field scores higher than a TRL 9 incremental improvement. But a first-ever TRL 7 deployment of something that's been stuck at TRL 4 for years also scores high.
5. **Keep ALL items that pass a minimum relevance threshold (>0.3).** Don't discard. The admin curates.
6. **Star the top 3-5 items by frontier score** — these get `isFrontier = true` and sort to the top of the admin dashboard with a ⚡ badge. These are the "you need to see this" items.
7. If a source has no usable image, call fal.ai to generate one based on the title + summary
8. Save all items to DB with their subcategory tags, TRL level, and frontier score
9. Once per week (Monday run), also pull the week's top community from SolarpunkList (highest Solarpunk Score among entries added in the past 7 days) and create a spotlight item
10. Log the run to `ResearchRun`

Over a week this accumulates a rich pool of items across all maturity levels for the admin to curate from, with the most frontier discoveries already starred.

### 3. Digest Generation (`/api/admin/generate`)

When admin clicks "Generate Digest":

1. Gather all selected items (where `isSelected = true` and `digestIssueId` is null)
2. Create a new `DigestIssue` record
3. Send all selected items to Claude with this instruction structure:

```
You are writing the SolarpunkDigest, a weekly email newsletter about 
breakthroughs in solarpunk technology: solar microgrids, wind turbines, 
rainwater harvesting, edge AI, permaculture, battery storage, mesh networks, 
3D printing, autonomous drones, and IoT sensor arrays.

Write in a warm, optimistic but grounded tone. Not corporate. 
Not breathless hype. Like a smart friend sharing what they found this week.

Generate:
1. An email subject line (compelling, specific, not clickbait)
2. A short intro paragraph (3-4 sentences setting up this week's theme)
3. For each item, a newsletter-style blurb:
   - A compelling headline (can differ from source title)
   - The TRL level as a badge (e.g. "TRL 3 — Proof of Concept")
   - If the item is frontier-starred, lead with a ⚡ and frame it as a breakthrough
   - 2-3 sentences of editorial summary (not just the AI summary, add context and why it matters for the solarpunk movement)
   - The subcategory tags as pills
   - The source attribution and link
4. A spotlight section for the featured community (if one is flagged):
   - Community name + location
   - 3-4 sentence writeup of why this community is worth knowing about
   - Link to their SolarpunkList page

Group items by their primary subcategory tag. Use section headers.
Lead each section with frontier items if present.
Return as structured JSON so we can render the email template.
```

4. Render the JSON into an HTML email template (responsive, clean, solarpunk-styled)
5. Save `generatedHtml` and `generatedMarkdown` to the issue
6. Show a preview in the admin dashboard

### 4. Review, Edit & Schedule

After generation, the admin can:
- Preview the rendered email in-browser
- Edit the subject line and intro text inline
- Reorder items via drag-and-drop
- Remove items from the issue
- Regenerate (re-call Claude with updated selection)
- Approve and schedule: pick a send date/time, or send immediately
- Issue status moves: `draft → generated → approved → scheduled → sent`

### 5. Send (`/api/cron/trigger`)

Cron endpoint that fires on schedule (or manually):

```
POST /api/cron/trigger
Authorization: Bearer $CRON_SECRET
```

**Send flow:**
1. Check for due issues (`status = 'scheduled'` AND `scheduledFor <= now`)
2. Verify sending domain (hit Resend API, check `SendingDomain.isVerified`)
3. If domain unverified: hold the issue for 24h (`sendBlockReason = 'DOMAIN_UNVERIFIED'`, set `sendHoldUntil`)
4. If hold expires without resolution: discard (`status = 'discarded'`, `sendBlockReason = 'DOMAIN_UNVERIFIED_GRACE_EXPIRED'`)
5. If verified: fetch all active subscribers, send via Resend batch API
6. Update issue: `status = 'sent'`, `sentAt = now()`, `recipientCount = count`
7. If no subscribers: block with `sendBlockReason = 'NO_SUBSCRIBERS'`

### 6. Subscriber Management

Simple for V1:
- Public signup lives at `solarpunklist.com/newsletter` (see Phase 5 build), which POSTs cross-origin to this backend's `/api/subscribe`
- `/api/subscribe` accepts `{ email, name?, source? }` with CORS headers allowing `solarpunklist.com`
- Double opt-in via Resend (send confirmation email with magic link)
- Unsubscribe link in every email footer (one-click, sets `isActive = false`)
- Admin can view/export/import subscribers from the dashboard
- No segments or groups in V1

### Phase 5: Newsletter Signup Page (on SolarpunkList codebase)

**Route:** `/newsletter`

**Design:**
- Full-viewport landing page with an AI-generated background video
- The video should be a slow, ambient loop of solarpunk imagery: lush green landscapes, solar panels catching light, community gardens, futuristic-but-organic architecture. Generate this using a video AI tool (Runway, Pika, or similar) from a prompt like: "Aerial slow flyover of a solarpunk ecovillage at golden hour, solar panels on earthen rooftops, food forests, gentle wind turbines, warm cinematic lighting, slow ambient movement, seamless loop"

- Centered content card (frosted glass / semi-transparent backdrop-blur):
  - SolarpunkDigest logo or wordmark
  - Headline: "The Solarpunk Briefing" (or similar)
  - Subhead: 1-2 sentences explaining what the newsletter is. Something like "A weekly digest of breakthroughs in regenerative communities, green tech, and the solarpunk movement. Curated by AI. Edited by humans."
  - Email input field (clean, large, prominent)
  - "Subscribe" button (solarpunk green, prominent)
  - Small privacy note: "No spam. Unsubscribe anytime."
- Mobile responsive: video scales, card stacks vertically, input stays usable

**On submit:**
- POST to the Replit newsletter backend's `/api/subscribe` endpoint (cross-origin, so configure CORS on the Replit app)
- The subscribe endpoint creates the subscriber record and triggers a double opt-in confirmation email via Resend
- On success: redirect to `/newsletter/success`

**Success page (`/newsletter/success`):**
- Same background video, same overlay
- Centered card with:
  - Checkmark icon (animated, subtle)
  - "You're in!" headline
  - "Check your email to confirm your subscription."
  - After 5 seconds, auto-redirect to `solarpunklist.com` (the main directory homepage)
  - Also show a "Back to SolarpunkList →" link for immediate click

**Technical notes:**
- The background video should be a short MP4 (10-15 seconds, seamless loop, compressed to <5MB for fast load)
- Use `<video autoPlay muted loop playsInline>` with a poster image fallback for slow connections
- The poster image is a still frame from the video (so the page looks good even before video loads)
- Add `NEWSLETTER_API_URL` env var to the SolarpunkList app pointing to the Replit newsletter backend
